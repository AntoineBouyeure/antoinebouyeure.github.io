# Antoine Bouyeure

I am a postdoctoral researcher in neuroscience at Ruhr-University Bochum (Germany), currently based in Paris (France). My work examines the neural mechanisms of memory, with a focus on fear learning and extinction, traumatic and autobiographical memory, and hippocampal function. More broadly, I study how memory representations are formed, generalized, stabilized, and transformed across experience.

My research aims to bridge fundamental cognitive neuroscience and translational psychiatry by identifying representational mechanisms that may contribute to maladaptive memory states, including intrusive and persistent trauma-related memories. To address these questions, I combine experimental paradigms, ultra-high-field (7T) neuroimaging, and computational analyses to characterize memory dynamics across the hippocampus and neocortex.

## Research Focus

**Memory representations and generalization.** I study how neural representations of cues and contexts evolve during learning, and how generalization contributes to adaptive versus maladaptive memory.

**Fear learning, extinction, and trauma.** I investigate mechanisms underlying the persistence, updating, and suppression of aversive memories, with particular relevance to trauma-related phenomena.

**Hippocampal function and laminar fMRI.** I use high-resolution neuroimaging to examine hippocampal organization and cortical–hippocampal interactions, including laminar approaches at ultra-high field.

**Translational neuropsychiatry.** A central objective of my work is to link representational dynamics to clinically relevant processes such as intrusive memories, resilience, and vulnerability to trauma-related disorders.

## Approach

My work integrates cognitive neuroscience, high-resolution neuroimaging, and computational methods. I develop reproducible analysis pipelines in Python, R, and SLURM-based high-performance computing environments, with a strong emphasis on methodological rigor. My long-term objective is to establish a mechanistic account of how memory representations become maladaptive in psychiatric conditions, and to identify neural markers and computational principles that can inform targeted interventions for trauma-related disorders.
