I am a postdoctoral researcher in neuroscience at Ruhr University Bochum, Germany, and based in Paris, France. My work focuses on the neural mechanisms of memory, with a particular emphasis on traumatic and autobiographical memory, fear learning and extinction, and hippocampal function.

My research aims to combine fundamental cognitive neuroscience and translational psychiatry by identifying how neural representations are formed, generalized, and persist over time, and how these processes may contribute to maladaptive memory states such as intrusive or persistent trauma-related flashbacks, avoidant behavior, or overgeneralized fear and anxiety.

I combine experimental paradigms, ultra-high-field neuroimaging, and computational analyses to characterize the structure and dynamics of memory representations across the hippocampus and neocortex.
