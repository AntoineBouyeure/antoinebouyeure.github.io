## Antoine Bouyeure

I am a postdoctoral researcher in neuroscience at Ruhr University Bochum. My work focuses on the neural mechanisms of memory, with a particular emphasis on fear learning and extinction, traumatic and autobiographical memory, and hippocampal function.

My research aims to bridge fundamental cognitive neuroscience and translational psychiatry by identifying how neural representations are formed, generalized, and persist over time, and how these processes can give rise to maladaptive memory states such as intrusive or persistent trauma-related memories.

I combine experimental paradigms, ultra-high-field (7T) neuroimaging, and computational analyses to characterize the structure and dynamics of memory representations across the hippocampus and neocortex.

---

## Research Focus

- **Memory representations and generalization**  
  How neural representations of cues and contexts evolve during learning, and how generalization processes contribute to adaptive versus maladaptive memory.

- **Fear learning, extinction, and trauma**  
  Mechanisms underlying the persistence, modification, and suppression of aversive memories.

- **Hippocampal function and laminar fMRI**  
  High-resolution investigation of hippocampal organization and cortical–hippocampal interactions using ultra-high-field imaging.

- **Toward translational neuropsychiatry**  
  Linking representational dynamics and neural mechanisms to clinically relevant phenomena such as intrusive memories, resilience, and vulnerability to trauma-related disorders.

---

## Approach

My work integrates cognitive neuroscience, high-resolution neuroimaging, and computational methods. 
I develop reproducible analysis pipelines in Python, R, and high-performance computing environments.
My long-term objective is to develop a mechanistic understanding of how memory representations become maladaptive in psychiatric conditions, and to identify neural markers and computational principles that can inform targeted interventions for trauma-related disorders.
